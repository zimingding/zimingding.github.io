<h2>Background</h2>
<p>I have been jogging for a long time now, but most my runs are 5ks. I find 5k the sweet spot, it clears my thoughts, loosen my stiff muscles, and is simply great exercise. The longest distance I had run before this was 14km City2Surf.</p>
<p>I did reasonably well in that race. My pace was around 6 minutes per kilometer, but I did not realize that 14 km at that pace was probably also my upper limit.</p>
<h2>Training and Expectations</h2>
<p>I did train for the HOKA Half Marathon. In a good week I would do 3 runs:</p>
<ul>
<li>one 4x800 threshold run</li>
<li>one 5k easy run</li>
<li>and one 10k long run in the weekend</li>
</ul>
<p>But as I am getting older, I am no longer as fit as the time when I did City2Surf. Naively I tried to chase a sub 2 hour finish, how wrong I was!</p>
<h2>The Race</h2>
<p>The HOKA Half Marathon has a beautiful course through some of Sydney's most iconic areas. Along the route are Sydney Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Darling Harbour, and endless million-dollar water views.</p>
<p>My start group is 7:35 AM. I arrived at Town Hall around 6:35, which gave me enough time to walk and jog towards the starting point at Macquarie Street in Martin Place.</p>
<p>Once I entered starting corral, it become very slow as all the athletes are in there. We moved slowly along Macquarie Street between 7:00 and 7:30 before finally reaching the starting line.</p>
<p>Then the race began.</p>
<h3>The Easy Part</h3>
<p>The first 5 to 10 kms are quite easy for me. The roads were not too crowded, we are running on the road with skyscrapers besides us. I ran with 6:00/km pace and I was with the 2 hour 10 minutes pacer. My legs are fresh, I felt great.</p>
<h3>The Turning Point</h3>
<p>Around the 14km mark, I entered a strange zone.</p>
<p>I had never ran beyond 14km before, my body began to feel noticeably tired, my pace dropped, the 2:10 pacer are long gone after Pyrmont.</p>
<h3>The Difficult Part</h3>
<p>I was tiered and the race still got a long way to go. The hardest section for me was around Barangaroo Reserve, its sand road and I am completely exhausted and walked for a long time. I think I had depleted most of my available energy.</p>
<p>If I had taken a gel at 12 km mark, the story would have been different. 14km onwards every km is a challenge, quite a few times I made up my mind to run for the rest of the course, but my legs simply refused to cooperate.</p>
<h2>Reflection</h2>
<p>I managed to finish the race at 2 hour 40 minutes. The 21.1 Km run burned 7000KJ of energy that is almost a small adult daily energy intake. Its a resilience and endurance test for me. After I got home, I devoured almost everything within reach and lay in bed for the entire afternoon.</p>
<p>I think the main mistake was not taking an energy gel, I wrongly believed that a half marathon could be completed without additional energy intake, the race proved me wrong.</p>
