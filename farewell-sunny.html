Farewell, Sunny

Sunny passed away on 30th June 2026. It was one of the darkest days for our family - my wife, my son, and me. Everything happened so suddenly that, even a week later, we still find ourselves in tears whenever we think of him.

I still vividly remember the day Sunny came home. We fell in love with him the moment he arrived from the breeder. That same day, We went to Petbarn to buy all the essentials for him. From the very beginning, Daniel and Sunny became the best friends.

There were countless moments that Daniel would sit on the couch reading a book while Sunny rested his head comfortably on Daniel's leg. As Daniel grew older and became busier with school, one thing never changed - everyday when he came home from school, and every morning after he got out of bed, he would stop to pat Sunny and give him a scratch.

Vivien loved Sunny the most. Whenever we planned a family holiday, the first thing she did was look for a dog-friendly accommodation so Sunny could come with us. Sunny traveled with us to Canberra, where we stayed in a pet-friendly apartment, and to a holiday park in Forster, and a farm stay in Kangaroo Valley. When we couldn't find suitable accommodation, he stayed at a dog hotel. After having Sunny, we stopped visiting national parks because dogs weren't allowed. Instead, we visited the Blue Mountains, walked along Dee Why Beach, and explored many other places.

Like every Beagle, Sunny lived through his nose. He loved food so much that resisting his pleading eyes was often the hardest part of mealtime. He would quietly sit beside us, never barking, simply watching us with incredible patience. Most of the time we don't give him any thing, but every now and then he would earn a small reward - a piece of bread, a tiny slice of steak, or another little treat.

Raising a dog is not a small commitment. Me and my wife took turns to walk Sunny every day, making sure he had at least 3 - 4 kilometers of walking. He loved spending time in the backyard but we soon discovered he would happily chew on sticks or anything he could find, so we limited his time alone in the backyard. Looking after a dog meant daily walks, feeding, brushing, cleaning, and simply spending time with him. It was a commitment of a couple of hours every day, which could be challenging when life became busy.

Thank you, Sunny, for six and a half wonderful years with our family. You brought us so much joy, companionship, and happiness. You will always be a part of our family and we will always miss you.

Rest peacefully, Sunny.
